This guide walks you through everything you need to go from a blank canvas to a published, shareable map. By the end, you will have imported a real dataset, styled it on an interactive map, and sent a live link to a colleague — all in under five minutes.

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have:

- A free Geo Web IW account — sign up at [app.geowebhq.com](https://app.geowebhq.com)
- A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari)

<Tip>
  Every new account includes a **14-day free trial** of the Pro plan. No credit card is required. You get full access to advanced styling, workflow automation, and team collaboration features during the trial period.
</Tip>

## Steps

<Steps>
  ### Sign up and log in

  Navigate to [app.geowebhq.com](https://app.geowebhq.com) and click **Get Started Free**. Enter your email address and choose a password, then open the verification email and click **Confirm Email**. You will be redirected to your new workspace dashboard.

  If your organization already has a workspace, ask your Admin to send you an invitation link instead of creating a new account.

  ### Create a new project

  From the dashboard, click **New Project** in the top-right corner. Give your project a descriptive name — for example, `My First Spatial Project` — then click **Create**. Projects are the top-level containers that hold your maps, data sources, and automated workflows.

  ### Import a dataset

  Inside your new project, open the **Data** tab and click **Import Dataset**. Geo Web IW accepts the following formats:

  <Tabs>
    <Tab title="GeoJSON">
      Select **GeoJSON** as the file type and drag your `.geojson` file into the upload area. The platform automatically detects the coordinate reference system (CRS) and feature properties.

      ```json
      {
        "type": "FeatureCollection",
        "features": [
          {
            "type": "Feature",
            "geometry": { "type": "Point", "coordinates": [-73.985, 40.748] },
            "properties": { "name": "Empire State Building", "category": "landmark" }
          }
        ]
      }
      ```
    </Tab>
    <Tab title="CSV with lat/lon">
      Select **CSV** and upload your file. Map the **latitude** and **longitude** columns using the column-picker dropdowns that appear. Any remaining columns become queryable feature properties.

      ```csv
      name,latitude,longitude,category
      Empire State Building,40.748,-73.985,landmark
      Central Park,40.785,-73.968,park
      ```
    </Tab>
    <Tab title="Shapefile">
      Compress your shapefile components (`.shp`, `.dbf`, `.shx`, and optionally `.prj`) into a single `.zip` archive before uploading. Select **Shapefile** as the file type and drag the archive into the upload area.
    </Tab>
  </Tabs>

  Click **Upload** and wait for the green **Ready** status badge to appear next to your dataset.

  ### Create a map and add your first layer

  Go to the **Maps** tab and click **New Map**. Name the map, then click **Add Layer** in the Layers panel on the left. Choose **From Data Source**, select the dataset you just imported, and click **Add to Map**. Your data points, lines, or polygons will appear on the canvas immediately.

  <Note>
    If your data does not appear after adding the layer, click the **Zoom to Layer** button (the crosshair icon) in the Layers panel to center the viewport on your dataset's bounding box.
  </Note>

  ### Style the layer

  Click the layer name in the Layers panel to open the **Style** pane. Use the controls to customize how your data looks:

  - **Color by attribute** — under **Fill Color**, switch from **Static** to **Data-driven** and choose a property from your dataset (e.g., `category`). Assign a color to each value or use an auto-generated palette.
  - **Opacity** — drag the **Layer Opacity** slider to adjust transparency. A value between `0.7` and `0.9` works well for polygon layers overlaid on a basemap.
  - **Stroke** — enable **Outline** and set a contrasting color to make boundaries stand out.

  Click **Apply** to preview your changes on the map canvas in real time.

  ### Publish and share the map URL

  When you are happy with the styling, click **Publish** in the top toolbar. Toggle **Public link** to **On**, then copy the generated URL from the **Share** dialog. Anyone with the link can view the interactive map in their browser — no account required.

  To restrict access to your team only, leave the link set to **Private** and share it from the **Invite** tab instead.
</Steps>
