Publishing a map in Geo Web IW converts your working project into a shareable, standalone experience. Whether you're distributing spatial insights to a client, embedding a map in a report, or collaborating with a team, publishing gives you full control over who can access your map and for how long.

<Warning>
  Public maps are accessible to **anyone** who has the URL — including search engine crawlers if the link is posted publicly. Use password protection or the Private access level whenever your map contains sensitive, proprietary, or personally identifiable location data.
</Warning>

## Publish Your Map

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the map in the editor">
    Navigate to your project and open the map you want to share. Make sure you've saved all your latest changes before publishing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click 'Publish' in the top-right toolbar">
    The **Publish** button appears in the toolbar at the top-right of the editor. Clicking it opens the publishing panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose an access level">
    Select one of three access modes for your published map:

    <CardGroup cols={3}>
      <Card title="Public" icon="globe">
        Anyone with the link can view the map — no sign-in required. Best for public-facing dashboards and reports.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Password Protected" icon="lock">
        Viewers must enter a password before accessing the map. Ideal for client deliverables and external reviews.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Private" icon="shield">
        Only members of your workspace can view the map. Use this for internal projects that aren't ready for external eyes.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optionally set a link expiry date">
    Toggle **Link Expiry** and pick a date. After that date, the link automatically returns a `403 Forbidden` response and you must republish or extend the expiry to restore access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the generated URL">
    Once you confirm your settings, Geo Web IW generates a unique shareable URL. Click **Copy Link** to copy it to your clipboard, then share it however you like — email, Slack, a report, or an embed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Before sending your link to anyone, click the **Preview** button in the publishing panel. Preview opens the published view in a new tab so you can verify the layout, layer visibility, and interactivity exactly as your audience will see it — without affecting the live link.
</Tip>

## Published Map Settings

After a map is published, you can control how future saves and edits are reflected.

<Accordion title="Auto-update vs. manual republish">
  Toggle **Re-publishable Updates** in the published map settings panel:

  - **Enabled (default):** Every time you save changes in the editor, the published map updates automatically. Viewers always see your latest work without any extra steps.
  - **Disabled:** The published map is frozen at the version you published. Changes you make in the editor are invisible to external viewers until you click **Republish** manually.

  Disabling auto-update is useful when you want to stage edits and release them on your own schedule — for example, preparing a quarterly report update without disrupting a live client view.
</Accordion>

## Custom Subdomain

<Info>
  Custom subdomain support is available on **Pro** and **Enterprise** plans.
</Info>

Instead of the default `maps.geowebhq.com` domain, you can serve published maps from your own branded domain, such as `maps.yourcompany.com`.

To configure a custom domain:

1. Go to **Settings → Workspace → Custom Domain**.
2. Enter the subdomain you want to use (e.g., `maps.yourcompany.com`).
3. Follow the DNS instructions to add a `CNAME` record pointing to `custom.geowebhq.com`.
4. Once DNS propagates (typically 5–30 minutes), Geo Web IW provisions an SSL certificate automatically.

All published map URLs in your workspace will then resolve under your custom domain.

## Unpublishing a Map

To remove public access to a map, open the publishing panel in the editor and click **Unpublish**. This action:

- Immediately invalidates the shareable URL — visitors who follow the old link will see a `404 Not Found` response.
- Does **not** delete the map or any of its data from your workspace.
- Can be reversed at any time by publishing the map again (a new URL is generated).

<Note>
  Unpublishing does not notify anyone who previously received the link. If you need to communicate the change, do so directly with your collaborators.
</Note>
